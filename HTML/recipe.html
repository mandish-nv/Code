Buff mo:mo:

Chef Ram

Category: Snack

Difficulty Level: Intermediate

Serving Portion: 4 servings (approx. 20)

Time required: 1hr 30min

Ingredients required

For the Filling:

  • 500g ground buffalo meat (buff)
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 1/2 cup green onions, finely chopped
  • 1/2 cup cilantro, finely chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1-inch ginger, minced
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon mustard oil (optional)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil

For the Dipping Sauce:

  • 2 medium tomatoes
  • 2 dried red chilies (adjust to taste)
  • 2 cloves garlic
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • Salt to taste

For the Dough:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup water (or as needed)

Procedure

  1. Prepare the Dough:

    2. In a large mixing bowl, combine the all-purpose flour and salt. Gradually add water while kneading until a smooth, pliable dough is formed. Cover the dough with a damp cloth and let it rest for at least 30 minutes.

  2. Make the Filling:

    3. In a large bowl, mix the ground buffalo meat with the chopped onions, green onions, cilantro, garlic, and ginger. Add the soy sauce, mustard oil (if using), turmeric powder, cumin powder, coriander powder, salt, and pepper. Mix well to combine. Add the vegetable oil to the mixture and combine thoroughly. This will help keep the filling moist.

  3. Prepare the Dipping Sauce:

    4. Roast the tomatoes and dried red chilies on an open flame or in a hot pan until charred. Blend the roasted tomatoes, chilies, garlic, and sesame seeds into a smooth paste. Heat the oil in a pan and cook the paste for a few minutes until thickened. Add salt to taste.

  4. Assemble the Momos:

    5. Roll the dough into a long log and cut it into small equal-sized pieces. Roll each piece into a thin, round wrapper (about 3 inches in diameter). Place a tablespoon of the filling in the center of each wrapper. Fold and pleat the edges to seal the dumpling. Repeat with the remaining dough and filling.

  5. Steam the Momos:

    6. Lightly grease a steamer basket with oil. Arrange the momos in the basket, making sure they don't touch each other. Steam the momos for about 10-12 minutes, until the dough is cooked and slightly translucent.

  6. Serve:

    7. Serve the steamed momos hot with the prepared dipping sauce.

Additional information

Nutritional Facts

Calories: 300-350 kcal Protein: 20-25g Carbohydrates: 35-40g Fat: 10-15g Fiber: 2-3g

Tips & Tricks

  • Ensure the dough is soft but not sticky, and let it rest for at least 30 minutes to make it easier to work with.
  • Mix the filling ingredients well to ensure even distribution of flavors. Chilling the filling slightly helps in handling.
  • Roll the dough into thin, even wrappers to ensure they cook evenly and are not too doughy.
